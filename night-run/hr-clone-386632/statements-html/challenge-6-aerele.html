Tara is tracking the daily price of a rare trading card over N consecutive days. She wants to make money by buying one card on some day and selling that same card on a LATER day.

Her profit is (selling price) minus (buying price). She may make at most ONE such buy-then-sell transaction, and she must buy before she sells (she cannot sell on the same day she buys). If there is no way to make a positive profit, she will simply not trade, earning a profit of 0.

For example, with prices [7, 1, 5, 3, 6, 4]: the best move is to buy on day 2 at price 1 and sell on day 5 at price 6, for a profit of 5. With prices [7, 6, 4, 3, 1] the prices only fall, so the best she can do is not trade, giving a profit of 0.

Given the prices for all N days, find the maximum profit Tara can make.

Input Format

The first line contains an integer N, the number of days. The second line contains N space-separated integers, the price of the card on each day in order.

Constraints

1 <= N <= 2000 0 <= price on each day <= 1000000

Output Format

A single integer: the maximum profit possible, or 0 if no profitable trade exists.

Sample Input 0

6
7 1 5 3 6 4

Sample Output 0

5

Explanation 0

Buy at price 1 (day 2) and sell at price 6 (day 5) for a profit of 5.
